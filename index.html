Mesa County Partners

Redesign Case Study

Problem Statement

Mesa County Partners was designed to connect new mentors with at-risk youth in order to provide them with a positive role model. We have observed that Mesa County Partners does not clearly display resources available for mentors on their website which causes new users to feel that they may not be supported through the process.

Solution

Redesign the homepage, navigation structure and key internal pages to clearly highlight all of the resources and support Mesa County Partners offers their mentors.

1. RESEARCH

Interviews / Survey / User Persona / User Insights

Many insights were gained during the research phase through the interviews and survey. We also gained tremendous insights from the Stakeholder of the Non-Profit, Mesa County Partners.

Mesa County Partners is a great nonprofit organization that offers three programs; one-to-one mentoring, restitution and WCCC.

www.mesacountypartners.org
Competition

Our direct competitors as we came to learn through the Stakeholder Meeting, weren’t competitors as much as just other Agencies working toward the same Goal, which is to help at-risk youth of today. For example, Blake Ammon, our Stakeholder Contact mentioned working with Mentor Colorado during the interview. Due to the nature of having a very aligned Non-Profit model, these agencies at times can offer each other support, resources and guidance.

Mentor Colorado

“At MENTOR Colorado, our goal is clear: to ensure every young person in Colorado will have a quality mentoring relationship. We’re on a mission to transform the quality and significantly increase the quantity of mentoring relationships for Colorado’s young people.”

Big Brothers

“The mission of Big Brothers Big Sisters of Colorado is to create and support one-to-one mentoring relationships that ignite the power and promise of youth.” Our Vision: “All youth achieve their full potential.”

Interviews

We as a group divided up and conducted user interviews to gain insight into the mentorship role. We conducted 6 interviews including a stakeholder interview which provided key insights into the company.

Key Message: Personal Growth

“The best part about being a mentor is seeing personal growth and knowing you’re making a difference.” -T

Key Message: Skills and Resources

“Intimidating if you don’t have the right skills needed to help a certain individual ” -A

Insights

Some insights into our interviews were that many felt unprepared to deal with intense issues that their mentee might have. We also observed that many were concerned with the time commitment needed to properly support the youth they’d be paired with. With these concerns expressed, almost all of our users interviewed said they had positive experiences, whether being mentored during their own childhood or making a difference in a youth’s life when they became an adult.

A valuable insight that we gained, across all users was how much support they would receive before and during a mentorship. Users mentioned orientation, education, and other supportive means to help them not fail at the mentorship they’ve committed to.

These interviews led us to believe that Mentorship support and resources would be critical to not only retaining, sustaining mentors through their interactions with youth, but also attracting people to considering becoming a mentor in the first place.

(Currently, on Mesa County Partners, 100 youth are awaiting mentors.)
Survey Results

After surveying 14 individuals, we found that half feel curious about mentorship, and half would be interested in mentoring. This information leads us to believe that people need more information readily available to them about mentorship, and possibly support before and during Mentorship.

Based on our User and Stakeholder Interviews and Survey data we created a persona that is interested in becoming a Mentor for Mesa County Partners Non-Profit.

John Landing

    JOB TITLE: Mechanic
    STATUS: Committed Partner
    LOCATION: Grand Junction, CO
    PERSONALITY: Thoughtful, Funny, Outgoing, Empathetic, Altruistic

John's Background

John is a hardworking individual with a stable life. He unfortunately got into a lot of trouble as a teenager and made some bad decisions that left him feeling very alone and lost. His mom connected him with a community mentor who ended up being a positive role model for him. John’s mentor was able to help direct him towards a more positive path which led him to find his passion for working on cars. John’s girlfriend has also always been a supportive and encouraging person in his life. John enjoys spending time with his partner, his close friends and wants to start making a lasting impact on a at-risk-youth in his community.

    Goals & Needs

  • John is interested in making a mentoring connection with an at-risk-youth in order to enrich their life as was experienced by him as a child.
  • Needs access to educational resources to feel prepared enough to take on mentorship.
  • Wants to be able to connect with other mentors-collaborate, share tips/information.
  • Wants to see personal growth in child/teen-witness them meeting milestones.

    Pain Points or Barriers

  • He’s concerned about his ability to pay it forward to a troubled youth. He was helped as a child, by a great Mentor, but to do what he did, John feels overwhelmed by the situation. Does he have the patience, love, compassion to help a child?
  • He was originally planning on trying to mentor last year (2020) however the pandemic made it very difficult to do so. John knew that so many children and teenagers were struggling and in need but he felt a sense of helplessness not being able to help in the way that he had originally intended.
  • John in concerned that he doesn’t have training or resources to properly mentor someone.

5. Final Thoughts & What's Next

Final Thoughts & What's Next?

  • Further Testing will need to be conducted to validate the new “Join us” button that I created. From the fly in micro animation, to how many times it appears through the user journey, and how now the Sign up will automatically reveal itself when you land on the mentorship page. Based off testing feedback, I would iterate further on the design. These new components could then be integrated into the Mobile app and Tablet version.
  • Rich content needs to be developed once the user does the initial sign up. The response via email and possibly follow up phone call will need to be just as positive, comforting and reassuring as the user progresses through the steps to becoming a mentor. This support also needs to continue as they are paired with a youth to ensure the success of both Senior and Junior Partner.
  • Continue with building out the Mobile and Tablet Designs - User flow, navigation, pages and testing)
  • Set up a content meeting in terms of lifestyle imagery, whether reviewing existing assets or plan multiple photoshoots to capture high res photography for the site.
  • Set up a stakeholder meeting with Mesa County Partners to present our Case Study and receive feedback. If well received, this Case Study could be used when a planned Redesign will occur.
  • Further iterating on content (Mentorship support and process of becoming a mentor) based on more user testing and feedback from stakeholders.
